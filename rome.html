You're most likely the Roman Empire.

In its heyday from 100 BC to 400 AD, the Roman Empire stretched across the Mediterranean Basin, into North Africa, the Middle East, and much of Europe.

Interaction Between Humans and the Environment

The Roman Empire utilized natural resources on a massive, unprecedented scale. With the expansion of Rome came the deforestation of large tracts of land in order to provide fuel and timber.

Development and Interaction of Cultures

Roman culture built upon Hellenistic, or Greek culture. This Hellenistic influence manifested itself in Roman art, theater, and religion. The Roman Republic worshipped a series of immortal deities under a polytheistic system. Religion was incorporated into every aspect of the Roman Empire, including its legal system and military.

State-Building, Expansion, and Conflict

Rome was an empire founded on conquest. They relied on more or less continuous military conquest to bring new slaves and economic regions into the fold. As a result, they got very good at incorporating the diverse cultures, environments, and political systems of the regions they conquered into the larger Roman empire.

Creation, Expansion, and Interaction of Economic Systems

The Roman Empire was the dominant economic power of the Mediterranean region, partially through the spoils they earned across their many successful military conquests. Roman merchants also sought profits in foreign locals, like China. Trade with Han China along the silk road proved to be an extremely lucrative venture for all parties involved.

Development and Transformation of Social Structures

The heart of Roman politics was always the emperor, the senate, and the wealthy ruling families. The power of the senate, however, and the social situation in Rome overall, changed greatly when Julius Caesar turned the Roman republic into the Roman empire when he declared himself emperor.
